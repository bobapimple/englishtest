English Homework - Home & Away!

Complete these exercises to practice your English grammar.

✏️ Exercise 1: a / an / the
Rule:
→ Use a before consonant sounds.
→ Use an before vowel sounds (a, e, i, o, u).
→ Use the for something specific or already known.
Fill in the blanks with a, an or the:
1. There is cat on sofa.
2. I have apple in kitchen.
3. She is in bathroom. bathroom is small.
4. He wants new bed.
5. Can you close window, please?
6. I saw owl in garden last night.
7. This is best room in house.
8. There's egg in fridge.
✏️ Exercise 2: Plurals
Rule:
→ Add -s: cat → cats
→ Add -es if word ends in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: box → boxes
→ Irregular: child → children, tooth → teeth, mouse → mice
Write the plural form:
1. chair →
2. box →
3. child →
4. tooth →
5. lamp →
6. dish →
7. mouse →
8. bookshelf →
✏️ Exercise 3: There is / There are
Rule:
→ There is + singular noun
→ There are + plural noun
Choose the correct form:
1. There (is/are) a big mirror in my room.
2. There (is/are) three windows in the kitchen.
3. there a TV in your bedroom?
4. there any chairs in the dining room?
5. There (is/are) no clock in the hallway.
6. There (is/are) a plant next to the door.
✏️ Exercise 4: Some / Any / No
Rule:
→ Some — in positive sentences
→ Any — in questions and negatives
→ No = not any
Complete the sentences with some, any or no:
1. There is milk in the fridge.
2. Are there books on the shelf?
3. There aren't pictures in the bathroom.
4. We have chairs in the living room.
5. There is time left — hurry up!
6. Is there sugar for the tea?
✏️ Exercise 5: Prepositions of Place
Rule:
→ on (на поверхности), in (внутри), under (под), next to (рядом), between (между), behind (за), in front of (перед)
Complete the sentences with a correct preposition:
1. The book is the desk.
2. The lamp is the bed.
3. The ball is the chair and the sofa.
4. The shoes are the bed.
5. The picture is the wall.
6. The dog is the door.
✏️ Exercise 6: The Imperative
Rule:
→ Give commands: Use the base verb.
→ Negative: Don't + verb.
Make sentences. Use the imperative:
1. (turn off / the light) →
2. (not / leave / your bag / on the floor) →
3. (put / your shoes / in the closet) →
4. (not / eat / in the bedroom) →
5. (clean / your room) →
6. (not / open / the window) →
✏️ Exercise 7: Present Simple
Rule:
→ For habits and facts.
→ He/She/It → add -s to the verb.
Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb:
1. I (clean) my room every Saturday.
2. She (cook) dinner in the kitchen.
3. They (watch) TV in the living room.
4. My dad (fix) things in the house.
5. We (have) dinner at 7 p.m.
6. The cat (sleep) on the sofa.
